Football Glossary of Terms

Football Glossary

Audible: quarterback makes a call at the line scrimmage to change the play that was called in the huddle

Automatic first down: When certain fowls are committed, the other team will get a first day automatically, even if they did not earn it on their own

Backfield: the area behind the line of scrimmage

Backward pass: when the quarterback throws the pass backwards

Ball carrier: fairly obvious, the person who has the football

Blitz: when you bring more players than normal to the line of scrimmage to rush the passer to create extra pressure

Block: act of one player obstructing another player with their body to push them back or prevent them from moving forward

Chain(s): the 10 yard long chains that measure for a new series of downs, usually stay on the sideline unless the ball is very close to being a first down, where they will bring out the chains to measure

Chip shot: a really short field goal that is basically given to be successful

Completion percentage: the percentage of passes completed from passes attempted

Cover: an attempt to prevent a receiver from catching a pass

Cut: Sharp change of direction by a running player

Dead ball: a ball that is no longer in play

Defensive team: the team that does not have the ball

Down: the basic unit of the game. First down is the first of the plays. A first down happens after a change of possession, after advancing the ball 10 yards following a previous first down and after some penalties. If a team gets the ball and is unable to move the ball ten yards, it becomes second down, if it happens again it becomes third down, if it happens again, it become fourth down

Down by contact: when a the player with the ball touches the ground with anything other than his hands, feet or arms as a direct result of contact with a defensive player

Drive: a set of offensive plays where the team gains yards, first downs and usually leads to a scoring opportunity

Eligible receivers: a player who can legally touch a pass being thrown forward

End zone: area between the end line and goal line, bounded by sidelines. This is where you need to get to score a touchdown

Fair catch: On a punt or kickoff, the person receiving the ball can wave arm overhead while the ball is in the air, signaling a fair catch. This means it is an unhindered catch and that once the ball is possessed the play is dead and the other team cannot make any contact with the receiver of the ball

Field of play: area between the goal lines and sidelines

Field position: relative measurement of how many yards the team needs to get to score. Good field position means they have less distance to cover, bad field position means they have more

First down: first in the set of four downs. A team has to move the ball 10 yards to get another first down, unless there is penalty that calls for an automatic first down

Flag: weighted piece of yellow cloth thrown by an official to show that a foul has been committed

Flea flicker: trick play! A running back throws the ball backwards back to the quarterback who then throws the ball to a wide receiver or tight end

Formation: arrangement of how the players line up for a play

Forward pass: pass that moves closer toward the opponent's end line than where it was released

Fourth down: final of a set of four downs. Unless the team gets a first down or has to replay due to a penalty, the team loses possession of the ball after this play. Team either attempts to get a first down, field goal or punt the ball to the other team

Fourth down conversion: Act of going for it (on fourth down). Team uses the fourth down play to make a first down

Fumble: when a player loses possession of the ball

Goal: surface in space marked by two upright posts and a horizontal crossbar. The goal is the surface above the bar and between the posts and extending infinitely upward

Goal line: front of the end zone

Gridiron: a fancy word for a football field

Hail Mary: when a quarterback makes a really long pass towards a group of receivers in the end zone in hopes of getting a touchdown, usually used as a last resort when time is running out

Handoff: player transfers the ball to another player by handing it to them, rather than the ball being thrown in the air

Hike: the handoff or snap pass from the center that begins a play

Huddle: when the players meet on field to talk about the next play

Icing the kicker: when the other team calls a timeout right before the kicker has the ball snapped in an attempt to make the kicker nervous and miss the field goal

Incomplete pass: forward pass that no player catches

Ineligible receiver: certain players cannot catch passes, like in most situations an offensive interior lineman cannot be receivers and the team will be penalized if that player catches the ball

Interception: player on the other team catches the ball

Kickoff: free kick that starts off each half and restarts following a touchdown or field goal

Kick six: when the field goal is blocked and the other team returns it for a touchdown

Kneel: a low risk play where the player with the ball kneels down after receiving the snap, ending the play but keeping the clock running. Usually done at the end of the game, when the team with the ball is winning to end the game sooner without needing to do a riskier play

Line of scrimmage: a line parallel to the goal line where the ball is to be put in play. Two lines of scrimmage are offensive line of scrimmage and defensive line of scrimmage

Live ball: any ball that is in play

Loose ball: any ball that is in play and not in a player's possession

Losing record/losing season: when a team's record or record at the end of the regular season has more losses than wins

Man-to-man coverage: when every player on the defense covers a specific player on offense

Motion: ordered movement of eligible receivers before the snap

Muff: loose ball that is dropped or mishandled while the player is trying to get possession of the ball

Muffed punt: when there is uncontrolled touch of the football after it is punted

Neutral zone: area between the lines of scrimmage

No good: what people say after a kicker misses the field goal

No-huddle offense: tactic where offense quickly forms near the line of scrimmage to begin the next play without huddling

Offensive team: team with the ball

Onside kick: play where the kicking team tries to recover the kicked ball

Open: when a receiver is separated from any defenders

PAT: stands for point after touchdown, also called the extra point

Pass: when a player throws the ball

Pass rating/quarterback rating: a numeric value that measures the performance of quarterbacks, determined by using the player's completion percentage, passing yards, touchdowns and interceptions

Passing play: a play in which a pass was attempted

Passing yards: the distance in total number of yards from scrimmage that a player has thrown the football plus the distance any receivers have run after catching the ball

Pick: another word for an interception

Pick-six: an interception that is returned for a touchdown

Play action: quarterback fakes either a handoff or a throw to draw the defense away from the intended offensive method

Play clock: timer used to increase the pace of the game between plays. NFL uses 40 seconds and the offensive team must snap the ball before the time expires or they will be penalized for delay of game

Pocket: area on the offensive line of scrimmage where pass blockers attempt to prevent defensive players from reaching the quarterback during passing plays

Possession: physical control of the ball

Preseason: In the NFL, preseason games do not count toward the final team standings. These games are used to pick final team roster and star players often have limited playing time

Pump fake: when the quarterback fakes a pass and keeps the ball in an attempt to trick the defense

Punt: Ball is dropped and kicked as far down the field as possible. Used to give up the ball after offensive downs have been used. This usually happens on fourth down

Punt return: receiver gets the ball after a punt and tries to advance the ball for better field position

Quarter: an NFL game is broken down into four quarters. Each quarter has 15 minutes on the game clock (but they usually take much longer than 15 minutes in real time)

Quarterback sneak: when the quarterback quickly snaps the ball, keeps it and runs right behind or beside the center. This is usually used in very short yardage or goal line situations

Reception: when a player catches the ball past the line of scrimmage

Red flag: weighted red marker thrown onto the field by a coach to tell the officials they want a certain play reviewed

Red zone: area between the 20-yard line and the end zone

Regular season: 16 games, excludes any playoff games and the Super Bowl

Return: advancing the ball down the field after a change of possession

Return yards: yards gained after a change of possession

Running out the clock: game strategy usually used by the team that is winning that involves repeatedly executing simple plays to keep the game clock running and bring a quicker end to the game

Running play: play where offense advances the ball by a player running with the ball rather than throwing the ball

Rush: a running play

Rushing average/yards per carry average: player's total number of rushing yards divided by number of rushing attempts

Sack: tackling the quarterback

Safety: a safety happens when a player with the ball is tackled in their own end zone, or when the offensive team commits a foul within their own end zone. A safety is two points for the other team and the team who was scored upon must kick the ball to the scoring team from its own 20-yard line

Scramble: when the quarterback runs out of the pocket to avoid being sacked, which gives the receivers more time to get open and the quarterback can then throw the ball or attempt to run himself

Secondary: refers to the defensive backfield, like the safeties and cornerbacks

Snap: handoff or pass from the center that begins the play

Snap count: “hut” sound a quarterback uses to signal to the center for the snap to be made

Special teams: units that handle kickoffs, punts, free kicks and field goal attempts

Spike: when the quarterback throws the ball at the ground immediately after the snap. It is technically an incomplete pass and stops the clock

Spot: the place determined by the official where the ball was downed or blown dead

Starter: first player to play that position within a given game or season

Stiff-arm/straight-arm: ball carrier pushing a defender out of the way by pushing them with a straight arm

Strip: removing the football from the player carrying it

Tackle: forcing the ball carrier to the ground

Three-and-out: when the offensive team does not get a first down on the first three plays of their drive and is forced to punt on fourth down

Throwaway: a pass intentionally thrown out of bounds by the quarterback while he is outside the pocket with no chance of it being caught by an eligible receiver or defender. This is done when quarterback is about to get tackled and has no open receivers to avoid a loss of yardage

Time of possession: amount of time one team has possessed the ball relative to the other team

Touchback: after a punt of kickoff when the receiver catching the ball in the end zone and taking a knee or the ball touching any part of the end zone, the team that downed the ball get the ball at their own 20-yard line

Touchdown: happens by catching the ball in the end zone or running the ball into the end zone, worth six points

Trick play: any play that uses deception to catch the other team off guard

Turnover: loss of the ball by one team to the other team

Two-minute warning: free timeout given to both teams when there is two minutes left on the game clock in each half

Two-point conversion: after scoring a touchdown, the team can attempt to gain possession of the ball in the end zone again either by running the ball or throwing the ball to get two points

Victory formation: team with the lead wants to run out the clock, so they take a knee

Winning record/winning season: when a team's record or record at the end of the regular season has more wins than losses
